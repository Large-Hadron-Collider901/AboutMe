Grounding Exercises

A couple of years ago, I really started to embrace self-care culture and began practicing grounding exercises. A grounding exercise is essentially any activity that helps you to focus on the five senses: sight, smell, sound, touch, and taste. them. These activities are particularly helpful for trauma survivors, as well as people who struggle with dissociation, panic attacks or anxiety. However, anyone can benefit from them. The idea behind this coping strategy is to pull yourself out of a flashback, anxiety attack, or even just an uncomfortable feeling and re connecting with your surroundings, by allowing your senses to bring you back to the present moment, where you have total control on your mind and body.Here are my top ten favorite grounding exercises!

  1. Hula-Hooping
  2. Lighting resin incense
  3. Weeding & watering my garden
  4. Polishing pretty stones
  5. Go outside & collect herbs from my garden, make hot tea with them (I LOVE the scent of fresh herbs!!) :-)
  6. Make coffee in my french press (Also smells amazing, great for sensory stimulation
  7. Drawing/Coloring Mandalas;
  8. Visit a botanic garden!
  9. Cook a delicious meal
  10. Resin casting
